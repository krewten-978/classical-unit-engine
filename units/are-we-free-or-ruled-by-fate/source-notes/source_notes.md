# Source Notes: Are We Free, or Are We Ruled by Fate?

## Reading 1: Aristotle on Voluntary Action

- **Student packet:** `student-packets/01_aristotle_voluntary_action_handout.tex`
- **PDF:** `student-packets/01_aristotle_voluntary_action_handout.pdf`
- **Source text:** Aristotle, _Nicomachean Ethics_, Book III, chapters 1--5.
- **Translation basis:** F. H. Peters public-domain translation, Standard Ebooks edition.
- **Stable source:** https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/aristotle/nicomachean-ethics/f-h-peters/text/book-3
- **Adaptation mode:** Modernized prose for 10th-grade access. The handout preserves Aristotle's sequence, main distinctions, and examples while simplifying syntax and adding student-facing vocabulary and tasks.
- **Unit function:** Foundation reading for praise, blame, pardon, compulsion, ignorance, choice, deliberation, habit, and responsibility.

## Reading 2: Cicero on Fate and Responsibility

- **Student packet:** `student-packets/02_cicero_on_fate_handout.tex`
- **PDF:** `student-packets/02_cicero_on_fate_handout.pdf`
- **Source text:** Cicero, _On Fate_ (_De Fato_), selected sections.
- **Reference source:** H. Rackham translation page hosted by Information Philosopher: https://www.informationphilosopher.com/solutions/philosophers/cicero/de_fato_english.html
- **Adaptation mode:** Condensed modernized classroom adaptation. The handout preserves Cicero's main distinctions and examples: fate vs. nature/chance, causes vs. necessity, the lazy argument, Stoic assent, the rolling cylinder image, and prediction/foreknowledge.
- **Unit function:** Adds a Roman bridge between Aristotle's voluntary-action framework and later providential, compatibilist, and determinist accounts of causation and responsibility.
