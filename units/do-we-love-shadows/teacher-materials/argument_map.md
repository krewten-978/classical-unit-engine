# Argument Map: Do We Love Shadows?

## Unit question

**If truth is good for us, why do people resist it?**

## Plato — The Cave

- **Central claim:** Human beings can mistake appearances for reality and may resist liberation because truth is painful and unfamiliar.
- **Because:**
  1. Prisoners know only the shadows they have always seen.
  2. Turning toward light hurts at first.
  3. Those still in the cave may mock or attack the one who returns.
- **Assumes:** Education requires a turning of the whole soul, not merely adding information.
- **What this view explains well:** Why people cling to familiar illusions.
- **Possible weakness:** It can make ignorance seem mainly accidental rather than moral or political.
- **Best unit companion:** Bacon names the distortions; Douglass shows enforced darkness.

## Bacon — The Four Idols

- **Central claim:** The mind is distorted by built-in human weakness, private bias, language, and inherited systems.
- **Because:**
  1. The human mind is like a crooked mirror.
  2. Personal caves shape perception.
  3. Words and systems can trap thought.
- **Assumes:** Better thinking requires diagnosing false images before pursuing truth.
- **What this view explains well:** Why intelligent people still reason badly.
- **Possible weakness:** It may understate the role of desire, sin, and power.
- **Best unit companion:** Augustine adds disordered love; Douglass adds social control.

## Augustine — Disordered Love

- **Central claim:** People resist truth because their loves are disordered; they may prefer praise, pleasure, habit, or self-image to healing.
- **Because:**
  1. The heart is restless when it seeks ultimate happiness in lesser things.
  2. Habit can become a chain.
  3. Truth asks not only for new ideas but for conversion of desire.
- **Assumes:** Knowing the truth is not enough if the will loves something else more.
- **What this view explains well:** Why people knowingly avoid what is good.
- **Possible weakness:** It can focus so much on the soul that public systems of ignorance need separate attention.
- **Best unit companion:** Douglass shows darkness enforced by power.

## Douglass — Literacy and Liberation

- **Central claim:** Knowledge can liberate, which is why unjust power often tries to keep people ignorant.
- **Because:**
  1. Douglass's master fears literacy because it makes slavery harder to maintain.
  2. Reading awakens Douglass to the injustice of slavery.
  3. Literacy gives language, consciousness, and tools for freedom.
- **Assumes:** Education is not neutral; it can threaten systems of domination.
- **What this view explains well:** Why some shadows are imposed, not merely chosen.
- **Possible weakness:** It needs Plato/Augustine/Bacon to explain why liberated people may still resist truth in other forms.
- **Best unit companion:** Plato's cave becomes historical and political.

## Newman — Knowledge Its Own End

- **Central claim:** Liberal education forms a free, cultivated mind; knowledge is valuable beyond immediate utility.
- **Because:**
  1. The mind needs cultivation, judgment, and enlargement.
  2. Job training alone may make a person useful without making him free.
  3. Liberal knowledge helps people see relationships and resist narrowness.
- **Assumes:** Education should form the whole intellect, not merely produce practical outcomes.
- **What this view explains well:** Why learning philosophy, literature, history, and theology matters.
- **Possible weakness:** Students may ask how liberal knowledge relates to practical life.
- **Best unit companion:** Douglass proves knowledge has real consequences; Bacon shows why trained judgment matters.

## Major positions students can defend

### Position 1: People resist truth because truth hurts
- Strongest support: Plato, Augustine, Douglass
- Possible objection: Bacon would say the problem may begin before pain, in distortion.

### Position 2: People resist truth because their minds are distorted
- Strongest support: Bacon, Plato, Newman
- Possible objection: Augustine would say the will and loves matter more than intellect alone.

### Position 3: People resist truth because they love shadows
- Strongest support: Augustine, Plato
- Possible objection: Douglass would say some people are kept in shadows by force.

### Position 4: People resist truth because power benefits from ignorance
- Strongest support: Douglass, Plato
- Possible objection: Bacon and Augustine show that people can distort or avoid truth even without external oppression.

### Position 5: Education must form the whole person
- Strongest support: Newman, Plato, Augustine
- Possible objection: Douglass reminds us that education also requires access and justice.
