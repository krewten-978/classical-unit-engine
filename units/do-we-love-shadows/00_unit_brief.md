# Unit Brief: Do We Love Shadows?

## Working title

**Do We Love Shadows?**

## Subtitle

Education, Illusion, and the Difficult Search for Truth

## Core question

**If truth is good for us, why do people resist it?**

## Student-facing question

**Why do people sometimes love shadows more than truth?**

## Grade fit

Designed for Wayne's 10th/12th grade classical English/philosophy classroom. The readings are serious but supported through vocabulary, student-facing introductions, modernized prose, and student-produced exercises.

## Final student product

Argument paper answering the unit question using at least two unit texts and responding to one serious opposing view.

## Unit thesis

Education is not merely the transfer of information. It is the difficult work of turning the mind, habits, loves, imagination, and judgment away from comfortable illusions and toward truth.

## Lean source ladder

1. Plato — shadows and ascent
2. Bacon — idols and mental distortion
3. Augustine — divided will and disordered love
4. Douglass — enforced ignorance and liberation through literacy
5. Newman — liberal knowledge and freedom of mind
