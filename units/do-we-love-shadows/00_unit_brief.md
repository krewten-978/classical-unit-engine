# Unit Brief: Do We Love Shadows?

## Working title

**Do We Love Shadows?**

## Subtitle

Education, Illusion, and the Difficult Search for Truth

## Core question

**Do human beings really want truth, or do we prefer comforting shadows?**

## Supporting teacher-facing question

**If truth is good for us, why do people resist it, distort it, or become attracted to beautiful illusions?**

## Grade fit

Designed for Wayne's 10th grade classical English/philosophy classroom, with enough seriousness to adapt upward for 12th grade. The readings are substantial but supported through vocabulary, student-facing introductions, faithful modernization, and student-produced exercises.

## Final student product

Comparative philosophical essay answering the unit question using at least two unit texts and responding to one serious opposing view.

## Unit thesis

Education is not merely the transfer of information. It is the difficult work of turning the mind, habits, loves, imagination, and judgment away from comfortable or beautiful illusions and toward truth.

## Revised source ladder

1. Plato — shadows, ascent, and the painful turn toward reality
2. Homer — the Sirens as beautiful, knowledge-shaped temptation
3. Bacon — idols and mental distortion
4. Augustine — divided will and disordered love
5. Kant — enlightenment, courage, and self-chosen immaturity
6. Nietzsche — truth, metaphor, and suspicion about what humans call truth
