# Source Notes: Do We Love Shadows?

These notes identify the source base for the student-facing adapted readings.

## Plato

- **Work:** *Republic*, Book VII, Allegory of the Cave, 514a–520a
- **Common public-domain translation:** Benjamin Jowett
- **Use in unit:** Master image for education as turning from shadows toward truth.
- **Student packet mode:** Modernized prose adaptation.
- **Current packet:** `student-packets/01_plato_cave_handout.tex` and `.pdf`.

## Homer

- **Work:** *Odyssey*, Book XII, the Sirens episode
- **Public-domain translation base:** Samuel Butler
- **Online source checked:** Wikisource, “The Odyssey (Butler)/Book XII”
- **Use in unit:** First literary illumination after Plato. The Sirens show that illusion can be beautiful, persuasive, and knowledge-shaped: Odysseus wants to hear what he knows may destroy him.
- **Student packet mode:** Focused long-work excerpt strategy using Circe's warning, Odysseus's instructions, the Sirens' song, and the crew binding Odysseus more tightly when he asks to be released.
- **Current packet:** `student-packets/02_homer_sirens_handout.tex` and `.pdf`.

## Francis Bacon

- **Work:** *Novum Organum*, Book I, especially Aphorisms 38–44 and nearby aphorisms on idols.
- **Use in unit:** Diagnostic framework for mental distortion.
- **Student packet mode:** Faithful modernized version of Aphorisms 38–44, preserving Bacon's sequence and fourfold structure.
- **Current packet:** `student-packets/03_bacon_four_idols_handout.tex` and `.pdf`.

## Augustine

- **Work:** *Confessions*, Book VIII, chapters 7, 9, 11, and 12.
- **Use in unit:** Divided will, disordered love, habit as bondage, and resistance to conversion.
- **Student packet mode:** Long-work excerpt strategy: brief front-context summary followed by a faithful modernized version of Augustine's focused garden crisis.
- **Current packet:** `student-packets/04_augustine_divided_will_handout.tex` and `.pdf`.

## Immanuel Kant

- **Work:** “An Answer to the Question: What Is Enlightenment?”
- **Use in unit:** Post-1700 challenge: immaturity can be comfortable and self-chosen; enlightenment requires courage to use one's own reason.
- **Student packet mode:** Focused modernized version preserving Kant's definition of enlightenment, the problem of guardians, the public/private use of reason distinction, and the claim that enlightenment requires freedom.
- **Source checked:** Internet Modern History Sourcebook, “Immanuel Kant: What is Enlightenment?” and LibreTexts public-domain page.
- **Current packet:** `student-packets/05_kant_enlightenment_courage_handout.tex` and `.pdf`.

## Friedrich Nietzsche

- **Work:** “On Truth and Lies in a Nonmoral Sense”
- **Use in unit:** Modern complication: what human beings call truth may be metaphor, convention, and image-making forgotten as such.
- **Student packet mode:** Not yet built. Use focused excerpts with strong vocabulary and context support.

## Adaptation principle

The student packets are not presented as full scholarly translations. They are classroom-facing adapted readings meant to preserve each author's core argument, sequence, and images while reducing archaic syntax and vocabulary load.
