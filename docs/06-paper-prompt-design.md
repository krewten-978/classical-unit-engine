# Paper Prompt Design Standard

A CUE paper prompt should require argument, textual evidence, and fair treatment of an opposing view.

## Required elements

Every full paper prompt should include:

1. central question
2. task statement
3. possible positions
4. text requirements
5. counterargument requirement
6. thesis expectation
7. conclusion expectation

## Prompt types

### 1. Defend one thinker against another

Example: Defend Aristotle’s view of voluntary action against Hobbes’s account of liberty and necessity.

### 2. Choose the strongest account

Example: Which thinker gives the strongest account of human freedom?

### 3. Apply a thinker to a literary text

Example: Does Macbeth act freely, or is he driven by fate, ambition, and circumstance?

### 4. Resolve a tension

Example: Can moral responsibility survive if every action has a cause?

### 5. Judge a historical conflict

Example: Which side gives the stronger account of legitimate authority and rebellion?

## Avoid report prompts

Weak:

- “Write about Aristotle and Hobbes.”

Stronger:

- “Which thinker gives a better account of moral responsibility: Aristotle or Hobbes? Defend your answer using both texts and respond fairly to the opposing view.”

## Thesis standards

A good student thesis should:

- answer the prompt directly
- take a defensible position
- name the main reason
- imply an opposing view

Example:

“Aristotle gives a stronger account of responsibility than Hobbes because he explains how character, choice, and knowledge make praise and blame meaningful, even when circumstances influence action.”
